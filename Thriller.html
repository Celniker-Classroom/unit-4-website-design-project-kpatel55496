Mystery and Thriller Book Reccommendations

Welcome to Mystery and Thriller! Below you will find some of my reccommendations below.

A Good Girl's Guide to Murder


(Click on the image to find the amazon link to the book!)

A Good Girl's Guide to Murder by Holly Jackson follows high school senior Pippa Fitz-Amobi as she reinvestigates a closed five-year-old murder-suicide case for her final project, convinced that the alleged killer, Sal Singh, was innocent. As Pip digs deeper into the dark secrets of her small town, she puts her own life in danger to uncover the truth and clear a forgotten name. One should read this book for its addictive, fast-paced narrative that uses a unique mix of case files and interviews to create a suspenseful, edge-of-your-seat thriller that is impossible to put down.

The Inheritance Games


(Click on the image to find the amazon link to the book!)

The Inheritance Games by Jennifer Lynn Barnes follows Avery Grambs, a17-year-old girl who unexpectedly inherits billions from a stranger, forcing her to move into a secret-passage-filled mansion to figure out why. To claim the fortune, she must survive a high-stakes, "Knives Out"-style game orchestrated by the deceased billionaire against his disinherited, suspicious grandsons. You should read this book for its fast-paced, addictive blend of complex riddles, familial secrets, and romantic tension, making it an unputdownable thriller that keeps you guessing until the very end.

One of Us is Lying


(Click on the image to find the amazon link to the book!)

One of Us Is Lying by Karen M. McManus is a fast-paced YA thriller where four students become suspects in a murder investigation after a classmate dies during detention. It explores the hidden lives of "The Brain," "The Beauty," "The Athlete," and "The Criminal" as they work together to uncover the truth and clear their names. This gripping, suspenseful "whodunnit" is a must-read for its clever plot twists and relatable character development, perfectly blending a modern mystery with high school drama.

We Were Liars


(Click on the image to find the amazon link to the book!)

We Were Liars by E. Lockhart is a gripping psychological thriller about Cadence Sinclair, a wealthy teenager trying to recall a forgotten traumatic event that occurred on her family's private island during her fifteenth summer. It explores themes of privilege, dysfunctional family dynamics, and the devastating consequences of secrets through a poignant, atmospheric narrative that features a deeply unreliable narrator. You should read it for its beautifully written prose and shocking, memorable twist that keeps you guessing until the very end.

Truly Devious


(Click on the image to find the amazon link to the book!)

Truly Devious by Maureen Johnson is a gripping YA mystery that follows true-crime aficionado Stevie Bell as she attends an exclusive Vermont academy to solve a notorious cold case from the 1930s. As the past resurfaces, Stevie is forced to solve a present-day murder that intersects with the historical mystery in a complex, twisty plot. You should read it for its atmospheric setting, relatable characters grappling with anxiety, and the witty, fast-paced narrative that hooks you instantly.

The Naturals


(Click on the image to find the amazon link to the book!)

The Naturals by Jennifer Lynn Barnes is a fast-paced young adult thriller centered on Cassie, a teenager with a natural talent for profiling who is recruited into a secret FBI program alongside other gifted teens. The team works together to solve cold cases while tackling personal demons and navigating a deadly, high-stakes game with a new serial killer. One should read this book for its gripping, character-driven narrative, which features intense suspense, shocking twists, and a "found family" dynamic that keeps readers hooked until the very end.

Five Survive


(Click on the image to find the amazon link to the book!)

Five Survive by Holly Jackson is a high-stakes YA survival thriller that follows six friends whose spring break road trip turns deadly when their RV is trapped in a remote, no-signal area by a mysterious sniper demanding a hidden secret. The story unfolds in near real-time over the course of one terrifying night, forcing the teenagers to turn on each other as paranoia and buried truths threaten their survival. You should read it for its intense, claustrophobic atmosphere and relentless pacing that will keep you guessing until the very last page, making it a perfect pick for fans of fast-paced, twisty thrillers.
